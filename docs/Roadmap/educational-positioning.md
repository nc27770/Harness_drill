# Educational positioning — where this curriculum fits in the market

> **Status:** Documented framing of how Harness Drill maps onto the
> education marketplace, from graduate seminar down to short-form
> social. Not a build plan. Useful as input if the curriculum is ever
> taught, packaged, or serialized.

## Subject

Harness Drill is structured as a self-directed curriculum
([`curriculum.md`](../curriculum.md)) but the depth and breadth of its
material — 15 modules plus 8 deeper territories, the modality matrix
extension at Module 1, and the conceptual scaffolding in
[`treatise.md`](../treatise.md) and [`measurement-seam.md`](../measurement-seam.md)
— sit well above what most "AI engineering" courses ship today. This
document captures where it would land if positioned as a teaching
artifact across the education marketplace, with honest duration and
cost estimates per tier.

## Where the curriculum actually belongs

Two formats preserve the muscle-building intent. The rest transmit
content but lose the *qualification* property the curriculum is
designed for.

### 1. Premium cohort-based, sequenced as 3 × 6-week cohorts

The "build before framework" discipline only survives with
accountability, peer pressure, and an instructor who notices when
the deliberate-failure step is being skipped. This is the Hamel
Husain / Maven model. The curriculum is bigger and more rigorous
than most things in that price band today.

- Cohort 1: Movement One + Module 1 modality matrix — **$1.5–2.5k**
- Cohort 2: Movement Two + key Deeper Territories (1, 4, 6, 7, 8) — **$1.5–2.5k**
- Cohort 3: Movement Three + Layer-3 capstone build — **$2–3k**
- **Full sequence: $5–8k**
- Cohort size: 30–80 students; instructor revenue $200–500k per full
  cycle if run twice yearly.

### 2. Graduate-level applied seminar at a top CS program

The conceptual scaffolding ([`treatise.md`](../treatise.md),
[`measurement-seam.md`](../measurement-seam.md), forward-design in
[`limbic-design.md`](../limbic-design.md) and
[`limbic-image-video-generative.md`](../limbic-image-video-generative.md))
is academically legible. Most current university agent courses are
either too shallow (project-based) or too theoretical (LLM math /
RL). A "Harness Engineering for Agentic Systems" elective at
CMU / Stanford / Berkeley / MIT would slot in cleanly with no direct
equivalent in the catalog.

## Where it transmits but doesn't develop the muscle

| Tier | Failure mode |
|---|---|
| Coursera / DLAI specialization | Reaches 10–100× the audience but completion rates collapse without cohort accountability. |
| Udemy | Even worse on completion. The "skill credentialing" surface doesn't match the "build the muscle" intent. |
| YouTube | Reaches the most people; build-along ratio is ~5%. |
| Substack / Medium | Plants conceptual scaffolding (treatise + measurement-seam are already this format) but readers who don't code along get qualification *theater*, not qualification. |
| Instagram / TikTok | Insight clips can drive funnel; the curriculum's depth doesn't compress to 60 sec. |

## Hidden cost the table doesn't show

API budget. The Module 1 modality matrix work — especially 1j/1l
(video output) — costs real dollars.

- Single full sweep of Module 1 extension: ~**$30–80** in lab fees.
- Full curriculum, including evaluation work in Movement Two and
  Deeper Territory 6: **$100–300 in API spend per student**.

Any premium offering should bundle credits or partner with a lab for
academic credits (Anthropic Education Program, OpenAI Researcher
Access, Google Cloud Credits).

---

## Qualification vs ratification

The curriculum is **qualification**: it gives you the conceptual map
and the muscle to navigate any agentic-systems problem. It does not,
by itself, give you **ratification** — the scar tissue that comes
from shipping and operating a real product.

A teaching offering built on this curriculum should be honest about
that distinction. Cohort-based delivery can pull qualification close
to ratification by adding capstone projects with real users; pure
self-paced delivery cannot.

## Sharpest one-line summary

This is a **Maven-tier cohort course priced at $6–8k**, *or* a
**graduate seminar at a top CS program**. Anything cheaper transmits
the content but loses the build-the-muscle property the curriculum
is explicitly designed for.
