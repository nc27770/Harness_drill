# Harness_drill
Harness Engineering Specialization Resource

## Overview

This repository is dedicated to harness engineering — the design, development, and manufacturing of wire/cable harnesses used across automotive, aerospace, industrial, and consumer electronics industries.

---

## What is Harness Engineering?

A **wire harness** (also called a cable harness or wiring loom) is an organized assembly of wires, cables, and connectors that transmit electrical power and signals in a system. Harness engineering encompasses:

- **Schematic Design** – Defining electrical connections, signal types, and power requirements
- **Layout & Routing** – Planning physical paths for bundles through enclosures, vehicles, or aircraft
- **Component Selection** – Choosing wires, terminals, connectors, conduit, and protective sleeving
- **Manufacturing Engineering** – Creating cut sheets, assembly drawings, and production instructions
- **Testing & Validation** – Continuity, insulation, and functional tests to verify assembly quality

---

## Core Competency Areas

### 1. Wire & Cable Selection
- Wire gauge sizing (AWG / mm²) based on current load and voltage drop
- Insulation material selection (PVC, XLPE, PTFE, silicone) for temperature and chemical resistance
- Shielded vs. unshielded cables for EMC/EMI requirements

### 2. Connector & Terminal Engineering
- Connector family selection (Deutsch DT/DTP/HD, Molex, TE Connectivity, Amphenol)
- Terminal crimping specifications and pull-force requirements
- Sealing requirements (IP ratings, environmental connectors)

### 3. Harness Design Tools & Standards
- **Tools:** CATIA Electrical, Siemens Capital, Zuken E³, Mentor HDAS
- **Standards:**
  - ISO 6722 – Road vehicles: cables and wires
  - SAE J1128 – Low-voltage primary cable
  - IPC/WHMA-A-620 – Requirements and acceptance for cable and wire harness assemblies
  - AS50881 – Wiring, aerospace vehicles
  - IEC 60228 – Conductors of insulated cables

### 4. Harness Layout & Formboard Design
- 2D formboard drawings for manual assembly
- Bundle diameter calculation
- Bend radius compliance
- Backshell and conduit routing

### 5. Manufacturing & Assembly
- Cut & strip length tolerances
- Lacing, taping, and sleeving methods (spiral wrap, corrugated conduit, heat-shrink)
- Crimping tools and die qualification
- Work instructions and assembly drawings

### 6. Testing & Quality
- Continuity and hi-pot (high-potential) testing
- Harness test boards and automated test equipment (ATE)
- Visual inspection criteria per IPC/WHMA-A-620
- First-article inspection (FAI)

## Harness Drill Checklist

Use this checklist during harness design and review:

### Design Phase
- [ ] Schematic reviewed and approved
- [ ] Wire gauge verified against current load calculations
- [ ] Voltage drop within specification
- [ ] Connector and terminal part numbers confirmed
- [ ] Mating connector compatibility verified
- [ ] Environmental/sealing requirements defined (IP rating)
- [ ] EMC/shielding requirements addressed

### Layout & Routing Phase
- [ ] Physical routing path defined and documented
- [ ] Minimum bend radius respected for all cables
- [ ] Adequate clearance from heat sources, sharp edges, and moving parts
- [ ] Bundle diameter calculated and within conduit/grommet capacity
- [ ] Strain relief and mounting points identified

### Documentation Phase
- [ ] Harness drawing (2D formboard or 3D layout) completed
- [ ] Bill of materials (BOM) generated
- [ ] Cut sheet / wire list produced
- [ ] Assembly instructions written
- [ ] Test specification and test board defined

### Manufacturing Phase
- [ ] Crimping tools and dies qualified
- [ ] Wire cut and strip length tolerances defined
- [ ] Lacing/taping/sleeving method specified
- [ ] Work instructions reviewed with production team

### Test & Validation Phase
- [ ] Continuity test passed
- [ ] Hi-pot / insulation resistance test passed
- [ ] Functional test performed in system
- [ ] Visual inspection completed per IPC/WHMA-A-620
- [ ] First-article inspection (FAI) signed off

## Key Terminology

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| AWG | American Wire Gauge – wire sizing standard (lower number = thicker wire) |
| BOM | Bill of Materials – list of all components in the harness |
| Backshell | Strain-relief/EMC accessory that attaches to the rear of a connector |
| Crimp | Mechanical connection method for attaching a terminal to a wire |
| DWV | Dielectric Withstanding Voltage – hi-pot test parameter |
| Formboard | A physical or virtual board used as a template to assemble harnesses |
| Grommet | Rubber/silicone seal protecting wire bundles passing through panel holes |
| IP Rating | Ingress Protection – specifies dust and water resistance level |
| Loom | Another term for a wire harness or wiring bundle |
| Pigtail | A short, terminated wire end protruding from a harness |
| Shield | Conductive braid or foil around a cable to reduce EMI |
| Splice | An electrical junction joining two or more wire ends mid-harness |
